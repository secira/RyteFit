
NEET & JEE Mock Test SaaS Website – Product Design Blueprint
Assuming the role of a product designer, here is a comprehensive structure for a responsive, SaaS-based NEET/JEE mock test platform that delivers rich test-taking experiences, performance analytics, national benchmarking, and AI-powered doubt clearing—ready for commercialization.

1. Core Website Structure
Landing Page
•	Bold “Start Your Test” CTA, demo video, highlights of features (question bank, AI support, analytics). Keep the landing page very simple like Google. 
•	Info on NEET & IIT pathways, benefits of mock tests, sample dashboards.
Student Registration/Login
•	Email/OAuth/OTP quick signup.
•	Personalized dashboard on login: recent tests, analytics, leaderboards, doubt support.
 
2. Question Bank System
•	Subjects:
o	JEE Main: Physics, Chemistry, Maths
o	NEET: Physics, Chemistry, Biology
•	Sections/Chapters: Each subject subdivided (e.g., “Organic Chemistry,” “Electrostatics,” “Human Physiology”).
•	Question Attributes:
o	Section/Chapter
o	Difficulty Level (1–5, 5=Most Difficult)
o	Flag if appeared in previous year paper (Year/Exam Tag)
o	Correct answer, detailed solution

•	Admin Panel:
o	Create/import questions (CSV upload)
o	Tag by difficulty, section, previous year appearance
 
3. Test Creation & Student Flow
•	Stepwise Wizard:
a.	Choose Exam: NEET/JEE
b.	Select Difficulty Level
c.	Provide User to take test 180 minutes
d.	Based on the timing selected dynamically prepare the mock test

•	Responsive Test UI:
a.	Timer, progress bar, mark-for-review
b.	Navigation by question/section
c.	Completion triggers score/analytics panel

•	Full Exams Timing for JEE Main
The JEE Main exam pattern for Paper 1 (B.E./B.Tech) includes 75 questions in total, divided into 25 questions each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. For each subject, there are 20 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) in Section A and 5 Numerical Value Questions (NVQs) in Section B. The exam is conducted in a computer-based online mode, lasts for 3 hours, and the total marks are 300.  
Paper 1 (B.E./B.Tech) Structure 
•	Mode: Computer-Based Test (Online)
•	Duration: 3 hours
•	Subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
•	Total Questions: 75 questions
•	Total Marks: 300 marks
Subject-wise Breakdown
•	Section A: 
20 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) from each subject (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics). All 20 MCQs in each section are compulsory to attempt. 
•	Section B: 
5 Numerical Value Questions (NVQs) from each subject (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics). Candidates must attempt all 5 NVQs in each subject. 
Marking Scheme 
•	Correct Answer: +4 marks
•	Incorrect Answer: -1 mark for MCQs and NVQs
•	Unanswered Question: 0 marks

•	All India Exam:
Twice a year we will conduct an All India Exam where students from all over year will login and take the test at a given time. This will be Sample exam just before the Real Exam Conducted by the Government. 
a.	Choose Exam: NEET/JEE
b.	Duration:
	NEET – 180 Minutes (all subjects)
	JEE – 180 Minutes (all Subject)
c.	Simultaneous Login – Estimated to have 10000 student’s login
d.	It will be scheduled for a particular date and time and all students registered will be taking at the same time. It has to be a synchronized test so user cannot logout and login. Timer will continue for 180 minute and stop. 
e.	Fees – 500 INR per Exams
f.	Leadership Board – All India Ranking. First 10 Ranks will be rewarded. 
g.	Question Bank – Question bank for these exams will be separate so that Students can have tougher pattern. Admin will prepare these questions with the help of Education Experts.
h.	Test-wise scores, subject precision, chapter-wise breakdown
i.	National Benchmarking: Mean score and standard deviation compared to all platform users nationally
j.	Graphs: performance trend, accuracy by difficulty, strengths/weaknesses
k.	National, state, school, and peer group rankings
l.	Badge system for top performers
